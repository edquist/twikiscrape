minutes:

Storage Status and Plans: (see Tanya's slides in the link). Tanya reported that the group has 6 people with 2.5 FTEs of effort. Lots of issues with information gathering/reporting. A test done by Tanya passed at only 3 of 16 sites initially, a few others were fixed in short order once the problems were reported. Overall there is a usability and reliability challenge. Dan recommended that this be brought up in a blueprint meeting, now scheduled to take place on this Thursday. Michael and Kent both shared that they believe the reliable use of opportunistic storage was very important to applications and users in the future. LIGO has been struggling with this for some time now with its binary inspiral applications.

Executive Director Report: (see Ruth's reports in the links). Ruth reported that Michael Ernst has accepted to replace Torre Wenaus as part of the Executive Team. Torre is taking on the role of US ATLAS S&C Coordinator.

The OSG operations coordinator (Rob Quick is now attending the WLCG operations meetings on a daily basis. The OSG management has taken action to insure the attention and involvement in joint operations and issues, as well as decision making and follow through.

LIGO hit a new record of 160K hours/day with help from many site admins and the code rework effort of Robert Engel.

Discussion have been taking place with US ATLAS, US CMS and other OSG leadership to understand the response to the request to describe experiment dependencies on OSG.

Ruth described (see link) potential contributions OSG make in presentations to ASCR.

The technology to identify on a user basis what science domain to account is not available and will need further discussion and development. Roth proposed that this completed the scope of the action item 12 and requested that the council consider it as completed. The council did not object.

Action Item 7:

- Discussion of candidates for Executive Directors be presented and discussed at the August Face to Face 
prior to the subsequent March Face to Face meeting.

 discussion at the March Face-to-Face Council meeting
- Prior Executive Director provides support to the newly elected Executive Director/Deputy for a period not 
to exceed 6 months at no more than 10% effort averaged over that 6 months.

Additional Item:
- Extend current ED appointment until March 2011.

Action Item 8:

- Duration of the Chair appointment - 2 years

Horst reported, as the voting administrator, that after the extension period, the total count for adopting Action Items 7 & 8 came in as 15:0 in favor. This satisfied the voting requirements for passing the recommendations.

Kent reminded everyone that within the recommendation was a deadline of today (OSG Council Telecon in the month of February) for official notice of intent to run for council chairs.

Kent informed the council that Paul and he have held several discussion on the subject of running again for council co-chairs and will use this meeting to officially notify the council of this intent. Kent asked if there were any members of the council that felt that an extension for filing intent for candidacy. No council members expressed a need for this. Piotr and Ruth both voiced support for Paul and Kent running again. Kent told the council that given the current needs of the OSG in its future planning, Paul and he felt that it would be of value to the OSG for their continued role as co-chairs. Paul expressed concern over the longer term needs for identifying and developing leadership in the OSG Council, which may be a topic for discussion at the Council Face-to-Face next month.

Kent described the panel that the OSG Council has been asked to formulate for the All-Hands Meeting and the representation on the panel. At this point in time, Kent has heard back from all requests for panel membership except Patrick from LIGO. The Collective VO representation is planning to have Steve Gallo on the panel as Shaowen and Tom will both be unavailable.

Tom described the planning for the special session for Collective VOs that he and Shaowen are organizing for Tuesday.
