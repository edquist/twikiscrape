SE Support Discussion

Eileen: what is our model of support?

Paul: we had suggested that people ask the experts: Alex, Timur, whoever. In fact, we could imagine posting an expert list on our OSG SE website. But, I can guess that Eileen will worry that these experts will then be pounded.

Eileen: yes, this doesn't scale to thousands of users, and what about when someone goes on vacation? This doesn't seem to work for either the supporters or the users?

There are many standards... a mailing list that you send to rather than a specific name. The problem here is that noone has "ownership" when a problem arises.

David: there is a trouble ticket service run by the GOC. And in the future there may be funding for paid support in this area.

Eileen: how does OSG handle this in other areas?

David: for CEs, handled through GOC. We need to build up a community of expertise... Timur, Alex, etc. are not paid to support OSG directly, and are not volunteering for this duty. We would need OSG support engineers of community member who are stakeholders. Maybe some people (in the future) in the GOC could become the first tier of responders/experts.

Paul: on a slightly new subject... what about test sites (SRM servers) that new users could use to test their clients or their applications...

Surya: Will my application work with SRM, or more specifically, will my application work with an SRM-dCache or SRM-L-Store (and so on) service? The public infrastructure on the OSG. There will be policy issues, etc., that will need to be posted similarly to policies for CEs.

We are trying to get a "network" of these things for the UltraLight benchmarking project, I would be willing to post my experiences if this will help.

David: And maybe develop some into "reference" systems, which is what I would like to see.

SE Interoperability Meeting

Meeting Agenda and Presentations

  • Modification recommendations and WLCG SRM V2.2 Usage Agreement
  • Implementation Schedule

Timur: workshop was called "Grid storage interfaces" primarily for LHC experiments. Three major storage systems that will be used plus reps from all major experiments and the SRM collaboration. Before the workshop, we worked hard to understandt the requirements for SRM, and we had a prototype working document. At the workshop, we agreed on changes/modifications, then agreed on sequence of chancgs, implementation schedule, plan of work. SRM V2.2 will be next, will implement Storage Classes (disk only, tape only, both,...) agreed on terminology for describing those, are files available all the time (online) or after a request (near-line), space reservations. Interface needs to be fixed with working versions by fall with all major functionality for LHC running. This was main emphasis of workshop.

We also discussed the GLUE schema interface. This service does discovery, for SEs it should be able to find your SE and figure out its capabilities.

Andy: our SE is really only used by our local JLab users, so this is why we use V3.0. Our users had problems with 2.0. Since we didn't have to get the interoperability part going, we just decided to go to the new specificaion.

Timur: V2.2 will "back implement" many of version 3.0 features, so V2.2 and 3.0 are not that different.

Andy: its an ongoing discussion within the SRM collaboration, which has become preoccupied with many users wanting new features in V2.x but were concerned about waiting for V3.x.
