Third agenda item

Progress with SCEC (Ted), LIGO status (Tanya)

Discussion

No report from Mats. Ted is planning to contact him soon.

Tanya has sent the STG meeting summary and questions and have got the following response from Kent: 

Q. MyOSG is not a critical service, what happens with LIGO production  if MyOsg becomes temporarily unavailable?

A. Critical depends on definition and on who you ask. LIGO can not generate workflows
to run on the OSG without MyOSG (previously this was the case with VORS and before
that it was GridCat). We typically generate the workflows by hand on roughly a daily
basis. These workflows can run for a week using hundreds to thousands of worker nodes,
but the use of MyOSG doesn't occur outside of the first step of generating each individual
workflow. As we pointed out, Pegasus is the vector for LIGO to generate workflow for the
OSG.

Q. Current MyOSG content could be different from bdii content in case when site admin made changes but failed to register the changes in OIM. 
So, it could be better to switch completely to discovery tools to query information from bdii and query MyOSG only for RSV probes?

A. We have seen this before but it has not occurred very often. Usually we contact site
admins and they say "oh yeah, I forgot that I needed to do that" Switching is not a 
LIGO decision, but a Pegasus developer's decision. We must leave it to them to 
decide.

Q.If you still prefer to get all information from MyOSG are you willingto wait for 3 months or you are ready to install and use storage discovery tools to get missing information?

A. As I noted in our teleconference, LIGO has is developing the technology to use storage
resources just now. We are starting out small (one or two sites) and will gradually grow
as our capabilities mature. In the meantime, we have been able to communicate directly
with site admins that are willing and interested in helping to get the needed information 
directly from the source and "feeding" this in to Pegasus by hand to keep the development
of this new technology moving forward. This approach will work for the next few months in 
my opinion.

Brian has also noted that we should not only worry about the discrepancy between "actual information", information reported to bdii and information in OIM but also about mapping of OIM "names" to Glue Schema. He is assigned to work on that in FY10. Tanya has proposed to discuss it on STG meeting.
