OSG Newsletter, April 2010

Production Report from CMS

This has been another exciting month for CMS and our LHC colleagues, as we have been taking data at a record center-of-mass energy of 7 GeV for the last few weeks. With simulation and analysis jobs, we achieved a peak of over 13,000 running jobs. The LHC accelerator folks continue to work hard to understand the machine in order to ramp up luminosity, while the CMS physicists are very busy defining secondary datasets and central skim runs in order to be ready to present results in July at ICHEP 2010 in Paris!

~ Burt Holzman

New Arrivals to the OSG Project

We welcome new arrivals to the OSG project and a new member of the OSG Council.

At University of Chicago we have a new person supporting the Trash/Trash/Integration team—Nathan Yehle. Nathan has been working on deployments of cluster management systems targeted for the OSG Trash/Trash/Integration and Validation testbed clusters at UChicago using open source tools such as Puppet, Cobbler and Cacti. He is a 2006 graduate of DePaul? University with a MS in Computer Information and Network Security; he holds a BS in Computer Science from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute.

At Fermilab, we have been joined by Douglas Strain, a new member of the VDT storage team. Doug has already started tackling multiple projects, including storage RSV probes, NEES, and Hadoop. He previously worked as a consultant designing websites for JP Morgan Chase, Aircell, and several hospitals. Previously, he studied computer science and mathematics at Carnegie Mellon, and then quantum computing at the University of New Mexico.

From the University of Nebraska, we have a new council member, David Swanson, and new members of the support team, Adam Caprez and Ashu Guru. David serves as Director of the Holland Computing Center (HCC) at UNL. HCC provides supercomputing resources to researchers throughout the UNL system, supports a regional VO of the same name, and hosts a US CMS Tier2 site. Adam and Ashu are helping with OSG documentation and measurements, metrics, and reporting work. They will engage Nebraska researchers to utilize HCC resources and the HCC VO. Caprez has a PhD? in Physics while Guru holds a PhD? in Integrated Manufacturing Systems Engineering from UNL.

At UCSD there are a number of graduates and undergraduates from physics and computer science and engineering working with Igor Sfiligoi on scalability and GlideInWMS?: Jeffrey Dost works on the process monitoring package (proc_monitor) used in the scalability testing; Christopher Theissen is helping in the development of the distributed testing system (glideTester); Warren Andrews is helping with the administration of the OSG glideinWMS factory. Alison McCrea?, Tony Myers, Robert Chen, Tim Kang, and Eliskhan Sheripon are working on code for the glidein factory that will help detect (and hopefully prevent) errors from misconfigured Grid sites, on helping to develop tools to ease the management and on better monitoring tools for the OSG glideinWMS factory.

~Rob Gardner, Tanya Levshina, Igor Sfiligoi, David Swanson

 

 

OSG Participates at Condor Week - Part I

Paradyn/Condor Week is a five day annual event that gives collaborators and users the chance to exchange ideas and experiences, to learn about the latest research, to experience live demos, and to influence short- and longterm research and development directions. This year Paradyn/Condor Week 2010 took place from Monday, April 12th to Friday, April 16th, 2010.

Given the co-leadership of Condor and OSG by our OSG-PI and software coordinator, of course much of the meeting was of interest to and had presentations by OSG members and partners.

Besides the many informative tutorials and discussions, there are many interesting talks to read—I hope not the least of which is my introduction!

~ Miron Livny

OSG Participates at Condor Week - Part II

The glideinWMS group, including members from Fermilab and UCSD, met with their Condor Collaborators and presented results demonstrating the stable operation of their glideinWMS deployments at UCSD and Fermilab, showing over 35 million hours served from sites in the OSG. An NSF-funded project, CorralWMS?, was also presented, which will integrate glideinWMS and Corral to allow utilization of grid resources in both TeraGrid? and the OSG.

~ Krista Larson

Updated Graphs of OSG Usage by Science

A report at a recent OSG Council meeting presented an up-to-date graph of OSG usage over the last year by various science disciplines. The metrics are gathered by adding a science field to a standard gratia query based on either the VO field of science or individual users' fields of science for VOs covering multiple science domains, such as Engage.

Please view information about the process of specifying the scientific field for a VO or user as well as graphs showing current usage statistics per field .

The non-physics and physics usage of the OSG continues to grow. In addition, we are continuing to discuss and understand the needs and directions for activities on the campus. In fact, I recently attended the Campus Bridging Task Force meeting, which had pertinent discussion.
