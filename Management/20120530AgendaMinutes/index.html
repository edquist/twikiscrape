Testing

We've been making steady progress in improving our testing framework. Recently we have been focused on reporting results clearly so that we can understand what has happened with our increasingly large set of tests.

You can see the latest test results at: http://vdt.cs.wisc.edu/software_testing/results/ We're having infrastructure problems with Red Hat installations, so unfortunately we have to ignore that for now.

2012 OSG User School

The OSG User School is coming together well. Students have been accepted and their travel logistics have been completed. (Travel logistics have taken considerable effort.) Tim is finalizing the agenda. The instructors are:

  • Alain Roy (Local HTC with Condor)
  • Igor Sfiligoi (Distributed HTC with GlideinWMS & Security)
  • Derek Weitzel (Storage)
  • Greg Thain (Turning science workflows into grid jobs)

Alain is working with local support staff to set up the local infrastructure to support the school.

Tim and Greg are working on some new ideas for structuring the portion of the school that focuses on turning science workflows into grid jobs. This year we'll be doing less lecture and more hands-on scenarios. Students will form small groups and engage with a "scientist" (aka school staff member) to turn their science application into a grid workflow. The science application will be based on a real workflow done in the Spalding lab here at the UW-Madison.

Extending software triage duty

We currently have a weekly OSG Software triage duty: we rotate responsibility for initial user support ticket triage between all members of the software team that are at least 50% time.

I am planning to extend the responsibilities for triage duty to include looking over the daily testing results. This will both ensure the results are being looked at and will help people have a broader knowledge of what is happening.
