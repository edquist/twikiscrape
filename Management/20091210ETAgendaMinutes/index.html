OSG Area Coordinators Meeting

Meeting Coordinates

Date Thursday, December 10, 2009
Time Noon Central
Telephone Number 510-665-5437
Teleconference ID 1111

Attendees

Igor Sfiligoi, Chander Sehgal, Peter Doherty, Jim Weichel, Maxim Potekhin, Jeff Boote

Agenda

  • 1.8 Training and Content Management – Jim Weichel (confirmed)

  • 2.1 Scalability, Reliability and Usability – Igor Sfiligoi (confirmed)
    • storage testing
      Bestman+HDFS was extensively tested at UCSD. The results were presented as a paper at IEEE conference and are available as OSG DOC 911.
      Performed a benchmark using different levels of security options; CPUs seem fast enough these days that we could afford both integrity and privacy; see the test page.
      Now collaborating with CMS to get IO profiling of several OSG sites.
    • GRAM5 testing
      Deployed GRAM5 at UCSD on top of standard OSG CE.
      Benchmark numbers very impressive; 10x speedup compared to GT2. Load on the CE also much lower; the new operational logic should also prevent overloads due to too many jobs being submitted. See GRAM5 test page and GT2 test page.
      Still some bugs that lead to GRAM5 lockouts. Working with both Globus team and Condor team to iron out the observed problems.
      A possible problem without a current solution is the fact that PRIMA does not work with GRAM5 and there are no plans in the Privilege group to solve this before next summer.
    • CREAM testing
      Testing of a European instance worked fine, but low scale.
      Have tried to install a CREAM insteance at UCSD, but found problems; CREAM developers helping with those right now.
    • glideinWMS work
      Assisting CMS in their request for having a single listening port node in the glideinWMS installation. Discussion with the Condor team and testing of the per-releases.
    • testing frameworks
      Finished the testing and monitoring CE framework; repository at http://sourceforge.net/projects/osgscal/. In the process of finalising the packaging.
      Work started on a framework for distributed testing; the aim is to have a tool that uses O(1000) Grid slots to run controlled tests against centralized services (like SRM). Will be based on a flavor of glideinWMS.

  • 2.2 Work Load Management – Maxim Potekhin (confirmed): report - Powerpoint slides, PDF

  • 2.4 Internet2 Network Monitoring Update – Jeff Boote (confirmed)

-- ChanderSehgal - 10 Dec 2009
