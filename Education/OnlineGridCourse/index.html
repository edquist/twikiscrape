Self-Paced Online Grid School

We invite your comments and suggestions about this school. Please send your ideas to gridschool-content@opensciencegrid.org

Goal
to provide students with a basic foundation in distributed computing, and valuable hands-on training in grid computing techniques. The course introduces essential skills that will be needed by students in natural and applied sciences, engineering, and computer science to conduct and support scientific analysis in the emerging grid computing environment.

Materials presented here have been developed by leading experts in grid computing (founders and developers of core technologies, such as Globus, Condor, Swift) and include lecture notes (audio and/or video available) and lab sessions. The course will guide through the use of national cyberinfrastructure - Open Science Grid and Tera Grid - to perform large-scale computations and data-intensive processing that would ultimately be needed in various fields of research.

Target audience
Undergraduate and graduate students, researchers, educators and professionals in engineering, computer science, or any scientific, data- or computing-intensive discipline.

PreRequisites
Applicants should have at least intermediate programming skills (one to two semesters experience in C/C++, Java, Perl, and/or Python) and hands-on experience with UNIX / Linux in a networked environment. (if you do not meet these criteria, please follow first these Unix tutorials).

We encourage you to bring your problem or project to us, and we will do our best to match you with an expert for advice.


Topics covered
  • Overview of distributed computing concepts and tools
  • Concepts, tools, and techniques of grid computing
  • Discovering and using grid resources
  • Grid scheduling and distributed data management
  • Techniques for workflow and collaboration
  • Grid site installation
