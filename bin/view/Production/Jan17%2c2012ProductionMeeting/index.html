Security (Mine)

  • Operational issues/Vulnerabilities/Incidents
    • Had an incident reported on TTU past Wednesday. All grid nodes were compromised at root level. We think it was a brute force ssh attack. Attacker replaced the sshd daemon. TTU security team will have the final say on this. Incident is under control now; we moved on to eradication and recovery stages. we expect TTU to re-install the machines and get back into business soon. Affected users are already received their new certificates. OSG Incident response plan was enacted: announcemet to sites and users were sent; contacted each user and service who has used the compromised nodes during the attack period; continuous monitoring via gratia for proxies. Production was affected because the nodes were taken offline immediately. TTU security team is doing forensics analysis.
  • Software vulnerabilities.
    • Two important patch announcements. Kernel level vulnerabilities and libxml2 vulnerability. SL released the patch. We alerted our site admins on Friday evening.
    • NIST released an Important vulnerability with Tomcat and web application languages. We alerted VDT and the GOC team as they may run vulnerable versions.

  • New IGTF release with accredited production DigiCert? CA coming out soon. Preview bundle for testing purposes is released by IGTF. OSG will need to test this soon.
  • We still need to complete our testing of browsers with CA root certificates which has a short lifetime. This test stems from the issue that DOEGrids has a new certificate. This is described at our last meeting minutes.
  • Working with VDT and GOC teams to reconcile the CA distribution process. We made a plan of action. We identified the sites who uses GOC rpm.
  • DigiCert? pilot is going well. The final recommendation will be due to end of January.
