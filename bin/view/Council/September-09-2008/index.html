Establishing a "Value" Model for the OSG (continuation of Face-to-Face discussion)

Kent reminded the Council of the discussion at the face-to-face meeting of the need for OSG to establish a "value model" for the OSG. Basically, this should be a superset of an economic model that captures out values on resources, their uses and the scientific results that come out of the OSG. It was also noted that Miron will be taking a related request to the Trash/Trash/Blueprint meetings to evaluate how to recognize what type of science was being carried out on any particular OSG resource.

Scott said that he could think of two approaches to measure cost/value:

    1. Measure the cost of establishing all the resources on the OSG (not easy and probably doesn't carry much comparative value)
    2. Measure the resources being served and compare with the cost for similar resources to the commercial cloud computing providers (e.g., Amazon, Google, IBM, etc.)

No further suggestions were presented.
