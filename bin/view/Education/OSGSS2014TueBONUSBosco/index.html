BONUS: Connecting to OSG with Bosco

For this exercise, you will submit jobs to a queue on your laptop to have them run on the HTCondor cluster or OSG glidein server. Bosco is software that is specifically designed to allow this capability.

Pair Up!

Given that Bosco was created for unix-based operating systems (Linux or Mac), try to pair up such that a student with a non-unix laptop can still see Bosco at work on another student's Mac or Linux laptop.

1. Submit jobs to the School condor pool or to OSG from your laptop

Visit the Bosco homepage (http://bosco.opensciencegrid.org/) to learn more about Bosco, and follow the "QuickStart" guide to submit jobs. You'll need to use the hostname of the School submit node or glidein server.

2. Submit jobs through OSG Connect from your laptop

Visit the following page in the OSG ConnectBook? on using Bosco with OSG Connect: https://confluence.grid.iu.edu/pages/viewpage.action?pageId=10944561

3. Explore!

Try any of the following, according to guides on the Bosco website and in the OSG ConnectBook?:

-Submit jobs from your laptop to multiple other submit points (School submit node, School glidein server, OSG Connect server, your own cluster): https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/Trash/Trash/Trash/Trash/CampusGrids/BoscoInstall#7_Multi_Cluster_Bosco -Submit jobs from the OSG Connect server to another cluster you have access to: https://confluence.grid.iu.edu/display/CON/Submit+to+your+campus+cluster+from+OSG+Connect
