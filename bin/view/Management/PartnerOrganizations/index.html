Introduction

The Open Science Grid maintains a partner relationship with many other organizations that are related grid infrastructures, other high performance computing infrastructures, international consortia, and certain project organizations that operate in the broad space of high throughput or high performance computing. This page references active OSG Partners with links to basic information on the partnership.

OSG Partners

Below is a list of active OSG Partners with the:
  1. definition of the Partner, including a link to the the Partner,
  2. reason we are partnering, and
  3. OSG and Partner contacts.

CDIGS

  1. CDIGS is the Community Development and Improvement of Globus Software.
  2. The Globus Toolkit provides some core software underpinnings for the OSG software stack. OSG provides needs to CDIGS and wants to ensure that the development evolution of Globus continues to inter-work with OSG software. OSG works with CDIGS on the scalability and functional testing of new components needed by the OSG community.
  3. Chander Sehgal and Alain Roy are the OSG interfaces to Globus. Steve Tuecke is the technical lead of Globus and is the contact with the OSG.

CILogon

  1. CILogon is a project that provides an open source, standards-based cyberinfrastructure (CI) logon service, providing the NSF research community with credentials for secure access to the U.S. CI that bridges the identity credentials generated by the nation’s universities to a credential that will satisfy the authentication and authorization needs of NSF’s CI projects.
  2. Authentication and authorization are important components of the OSG. OSG plans to support authentication provided by the CILogon service and is working with the project to this end. The CILogin project is expected to provide a production service used by several of the OSG stakeholders, including one major account, LIGO. It provides a path to the future integration of OSG VO Identity Management with the Shibboleth System campus identity infrastructure.
  3. Mine Altunay is the OSG interface to CILogon. Jim Basney is the PI of CILogon and is the contact with OSG.

EGI

  1. EGI is the European Grid Initiative that coordinates federation of the National Grid Infrastructures (NGI) in Europe. This is an follow-on to the EGEE (Enabling Grids for E-sciencE) project.
  2. EGI is the peer distributed infrastructure in Europe and several OSG VOs depend on transparent access and operations of their services using EMI and OSG.
  3. Ruth Pordes and Miron Livny are the OSG contacts. Steve Newhouse is the project director of EGI and is the contact with OSG.

EMI

  1. EMI is the European Middleware Initiative. The ARC, gLite, UNICORE and dCache middleware providers have joined forces to support and evolve the software used by the European and international research infrastructures.
  2. EMI is the peer software packager in Europe. The OSG Virtual Data Toolkit software stack must continue to interoperate with the EMI software stack as the infrastructures evolve to meet the needs of several OSG VOs.
  3. Alain Roy is the OSG contact and attends the weekly Engineering Middleware Taskforce meetings. Alberto DiMiglio is the Director of EMI and is the contact with the OSG.

ESnet

  1. ESnet is a high-speed network serving thousands of Department of Energy (DOE) scientists at over 40 institutions.
  2. OSG VOs critically depend on the Certificate Authority service of ESnet. Additionally the data movement of many of the OSG VOs crosses the ESnet managed and operated network connections.

FutureGrid

  1. FutureGrid is a project to study the computer science aspects of use and security of computing grids and clouds. It provides a Cloud based testbed for NSF computer science researchers.
  2. OSG is interested in the study of use and security of computing grids as a core element in our mission. OSG provides a similar labortory for computer science researchers to test and discover computing at large scale. It is beneficial to researchers to be able to move easily between the two infrastructures.
  3. Ruth Pordes is on the FutureGrid User Advisory Board and is the OSG contact. Geoffrey Fox is the PI of FutureGrid.

Galois

  1. Galois is an R&D company that builds products to provide security and trusted collaboration for computer networks. They have an DOE SBIR Phase II award to provide identity management solutions for collaborative science.
  2. Although OSG did not participate in the award proposal, Galois sees OSG as the prime environment to deliver their new tools. We are helping Galois collect identity management needs from VOs and expect to test and evaluate the software resulting from this project for use in OSG.
  3. Mine Altunay is the OSG contact and coordinates interaction with OSG. Josh Hoyt is the Galois contact.

GridColombia

  1. GridColombia is the Colombian National Grid. GridColombia site in English
  2. OSG's Americas Outreach program promotes helping communities in Central and South America to build and operate their local distributed infrastructures. We are partnering with various universities in Colombia to assist them in establishing a national grid in Colombia which is expected to become a partner grid with the OSG.
  3. Jose Caballero? is the OSG interface to GridColombia. Enrique González, director of GridColombia, is OSG's primary contact.

GridUNESP

  1. GridUNESP is São Paulo State University's statewide, multi-campus computational grid. GridUNESP site in English
  2. OSG's Americas Outreach program promotes helping communities in Central and South America to build and operate their local distributed infrastructures. GridUNESP is a Brasil grid that will be a partner grid with the OSG. More information about GridUNESP's configuration can be found on the NCC TWiki as well as the OSG VO Twiki.
  3. Horst Severini is the OSG liaison to GridUNESP. Sergio Novaes is the director of GridUNESP and OSG's primary contact.

Internet2

  1. Internet2 is a U.S. advanced networking consortium of the research and education community of 200 U.S. universities, 70 corporations, and 45 government agencies and other institutions. The consortium provides leading-edge network development, deployment, and capabilities to its members.
  2. The data movement of many of the OSG VOs crosses Internet2 managed and operated network connections.
  3. Chander Sehgal is the OSG liaison to Internet2. Jeff Boote, the assistant director of Internet2 R&D is the Internet2 contact.

Magellan

  1. Magellan is a joint project between Argonne National Laboratory (ANL) and the National Energy Research Scientific Computing Center (NERSC) to build and operate a testbed for examining the potential for cloud computing for scientific applications. This is the DOE Cloud and Storage testbed for new technologies and end points for the 100 Gigabit infrastructure.
  2. Several OSG sites provide cloud computing facilities and OSG is also interested in the potential for cloud computing for scientific applications. OSG VOs are involved in testing and usage of Magellan.
  3. Ruth Pordes and Igor Sfiligoi are the OSG interfaces to Magellan. Susan Coughlin and Shane Canon are the Magellan contacts to OSG. The latest meeting is found at PartnerMagellan2010Dec.

NEES

  1. NEES is the Network for Earthquake Engineering Simulation. NEESComm provides a distributed IT infrastructure to the Earthquake Engineering Community.
  2. OSG and NEESComm have overlapping interests and expertise. NEES is a large user of computing resources and is experimenting with moving some of their computation to the OSG.
  3. Ruth Pordes is the chair of the Computing Infrastructure Sub-Committee of the Project Advisory Committee of NEES and is the OSG contact. Rudi Eigenmann is the contact from NEES to OSG.

NGS

  1. NGS is National (UK) Grid Service for academic research. It provides a National Grid Infrastructure in the UK similar to the OSG.
  2. OSG and NGS have mutual interests, goals, and expertise. NGS supports the OSG Virtual Data Toolkit as one of the middleware stacks used on its resources.
  3. Miron Livny is the OSG contact. David Wallom is the NGS contact to OSG.

NYSGrid

  1. NYSGrid is a collaboration among New York institutions to create an advanced grid infrastructure that will strengthen research and education capabilities across the state.
  2. The NYSGrid is a regional grid in the US and a partner grid with OSG. We work to understand the models and principles and where there is mutual benefit from adopting each others services or solutions.
  3. Ruth Pordes is the OSG contact. Tom Furtani, director, and Steve Gallo, lead software engineer, are the NYSGrid interfaces.

OGF

  1. OGF is the Open Grid Forum that shares best practices and consolidates these best practices into standards for distributed computing. OGF is the worldwide community forum for discussion of Grid standards.
  2. OSG is both interested in and a major contributor to best practices for distributed computing.
  3. Ruth Pordes and Miron Livny are the OSG contacts. See also OGF Active Groups for contacts.

Pegasus

  1. Pegasus is a project that encompasses a set of technologies the help workflow-based applications execute in high throughput and high performance cyberinfrastructures.
  2. As a primary workflow manager for OSG, Pegasus is an important partner and collaborator on projects. Some OSG VOs use Pegasus as their underlying workflow tool.
  3. Burt Holzman is the OSG interface to Pegasus. Ewa Deelman? leads the Pegasus project and is the OSG contact.

XSEDE (was TeraGrid)

  1. XSEDE integrates advanced digital resources and services used by scientists for research, makes those services easier to use, and helps more people use them. XSEDE supports 16 supercomputers around the US. A series of talks on XSEDE can be found at XSEDE Roundtable Meetings.
  2. OSG is a Service Provider under the XSEDE umbrella. Over time, we expect to better integrate the HTC and HPC resources in XSEDE to simplify use of the US Cyberinfrastructure by scientists.
  3. Ruth Pordes is OSG's primary interface to XSEDE while Miron Livny and DanFraser? serve as the OSG representative in the Service Provider Forum. John Towns is a primary contact to OSG.
  4. The OSG Campus Grid activity is working together with the XSEDE Campus Champions on providing campus researchers and faculty information about and access to local as well as remote resources appropriate to their applications and needs.
    1. First information for XSEDEUsers on how to access OSG

WLCG

  1. WLCG is the Worldwide Large Hadron Collider (LHC) Computing Grid.
  2. The LHC project is a primary stakeholder (via US ATLAS and US CMS) of OSG and OSG is part of the Worldwide Computing Grid.
  3. Ruth Pordes and Michael Ernst (contact) are members of the WLCG management board and are the OSG contacts. Ian Bird is the primary contact to OSG.
